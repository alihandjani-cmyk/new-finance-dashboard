рџ“– Methodology

Roll (1984) Implied Spread estimates the effective bid-ask spread from the serial covariance of price changes, using only daily close prices.

Formula: S% = 2 Г— в€љ(в€’Cov(rt, rt+1)) Г— 100, where rt = log return on day t.

Intuition: In a frictionless market, successive returns should be uncorrelated. Negative serial covariance in prices (the "bid-ask bounce") reveals the effective half-spread. The Roll model assumes a constant spread and a random-walk fundamental value.

Limitation: If Cov(rt, rt+1) в‰Ґ 0 for a given stock (e.g. positive momentum), the spread cannot be estimated and that stock is excluded from the average. The Abdi-Ranaldo (2017) spread in the AR tab overcomes this by using intraday High/Low data.

Data: 30 trading days of daily close prices via Yahoo Finance. Market average = equal-weighted mean across all index constituents with valid estimates.

рџ“– Methodology

Abdi & Ranaldo (2017) Range-Based Spread improves on Roll by incorporating intraday price ranges (daily High and Low), making it more robust for liquid large-cap stocks where the bid-ask bounce in close prices is very small.

Formula: ct = ln(Ct) в€’ ВЅ(ln(Ht) + ln(Lt)), then S% = 2 Г— в€љ(в€’Cov(ct, ctв€’1)) Г— 100.

Intuition: ct captures the position of the close price within the day's trading range вЂ” a proxy for whether the day ended at the bid or the ask. Negative serial covariance in ct reflects the bid-ask bounce, amplified by the full intraday range.

Advantage over Roll: Uses more price information (H, L, C) rather than close prices alone. Empirically outperforms Roll for highly liquid stocks and produces fewer missing estimates on trending days.

Data: 30 trading days of daily OHLCV data via Yahoo Finance. Market average = equal-weighted mean across constituents with valid estimates (minimum 12 days required).

рџ“– Methodology

Turnover Ratio normalises trading activity by market size. Raw daily volume (in ВЈ/в‚¬/$ billions) reflects absolute size вЂ” a large exchange naturally has more volume. Dividing by total market capitalisation shows how actively traded the market is relative to its size: a ratio of 0.1% means 0.1% of the entire market changed hands that day.

Formula: TR% = (ОЈ Closei Г— Volumei) / (ОЈ Market Capi) Г— 100, where the sum is over all index constituents tracked by the dashboard.

Why it matters: Two exchanges with identical raw volume can have very different liquidity profiles if one is much larger than the other. Turnover ratio corrects for this, giving a size-neutral view of market activity. Academic literature (Datar, Naik & Radcliffe 1998) shows stocks with higher turnover ratios command lower liquidity premiums.

Data: Volume is computed from yfinance OHLCV data for each exchange's index constituents. Market cap is the sum of (price Г— shares outstanding) across the same universe. Both refreshed on each full workflow run (4Г— daily). Currency is native to each exchange (GBP for LSE, EUR for Euronext/Xetra, USD for Nasdaq/NYSE, CHF for SIX).

Role in ranking: Turnover Ratio replaces Roll (1984) spread as the third of four composite signals, alongside Amihud ILLIQ, Abdi-Ranaldo (2017) AR spread, and raw Volume вЂ” giving the composite four conceptually distinct dimensions: price impact, transaction cost, relative activity, and absolute activity.
